The 2016 Edgington Lectures: Kit Fine

The 3rd biennial Edgington Lectures will be delivered by Professor Kit Fine (NYU) on Friday 3rd and Saturday 4th June, 2016, at the Department of Philosophy, Birkbeck College, London. Professor Fine will speak on Compliance and Command: A Truthmaker Approach to Imperative and Deontic Logic.

The lectures are free to attend and all are welcome.

When and Where

Fri. 3rd June, 6pm,
Sat. 4th June, 5pm,
Room B33, Main Building, Malet Street,

Graduate Workshop

In tandem with the Edgington Lectures, Professor Fine will lead a graduate workshop, which graduate students in philosophy and related disciplines are invited to attend. This will run from 10am to 3pm, on both days of the lectures, the 3rd and 4th June.

The workshop is also free, but we ask those wishing to come to write to edgingtonlectures@gmail.com to register.

The papers in the 2016 graduate workshop will be:

  • Stephen Duxbury (Cambridge), Mathematical Logic and the Reduction of Modality to Essence as Real Definition
  • Charles Jansen (UCL), De-fining Parthood
  • Samuel Kimpton-Nye (KCL), Necessary Laws and the Problem of Counterlegals
  • Hsuan-Chih Lin (Birkbeck), Semantic Difference and Compositionality in Semantic Relationism
  • Antonella Mallozzi (CUNY—The Graduate Center), The Only Route to Metaphysical Modality is A Priori. Modal Rationalism After Kripke
  • Siobhan Moriarty (Sheffield), Fine's Modal Puzzle
  • Kevin Richardson (MIT), Grounding as Reduction
  • Nicola Spinelli (Warwick), Global Accounts of Necessity
