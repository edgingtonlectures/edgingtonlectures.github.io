The 2016 Edgington Lectures: Kit Fine

The 3rd biennial Edgington Lectures will be delivered by Professor Kit Fine (NYU) on Friday 3rd and Saturday 4th June, 2016, at the Department of Philosophy, Birkbeck College, London. Professor Fine will speak on Compliance and Command: A Truthmaker Approach to Imperative and Deontic Logic.

The lectures are free to attend and all are welcome.

When and Where

Fri. 3rd June, 6pm,
Sat. 4th June, 5pm,
Room B33, Main Building, Malet Street,
Birkbeck College, University of London,
WC1E 7HX­­.

See here for a map. (Note that the graduate workshops are located elsewhere: see below.)

Graduate Workshop

In tandem with the Edgington Lectures, Professor Fine will lead a graduate workshop, which graduate students in philosophy and related disciplines are invited to attend. This will run on both days of the lectures.

Fri. 3rd June: 09:30–15:30, 30 Russell Square, room 102. (Map here.)
Sat. 4th June: 10:00–15:30, Room G15, Main Building, Malet Street. (Map here.)

The workshop is also free, but we ask those wishing to come to write to edgingtonlectures@gmail.com to register.
